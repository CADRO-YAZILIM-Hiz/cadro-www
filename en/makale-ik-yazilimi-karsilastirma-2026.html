An independent evaluation of leading HR platforms in Turkey across 8 key criteria including legal compliance, payroll, and data protection.

Editorial Note: This comparison is based on publicly available information and surveys from CADRO customers. Data for competitor software comes from public sources (official websites, G2, Capterra) and may vary. Our goal is to define the market objectively.

Why HR Software Selection Is More Critical in 2026

In Turkey, as of 2026, HR software selection is no longer just a productivity choice — it has become a legal necessity. Social Security (SGK) e-Declaration API integration, data processing records under data protection law (KVKK), and compliance with Labor Law No. 7036 — all of these create serious financial risk in case of wrong software selection.

Detailed Criteria Analysis

1. Legal Compliance — The Red Line in Turkey

SGK e-Declaration API integration, monthly withholding tax calculations, and stamp duty deductions are fundamental requirements of Turkish labor law. Foreign-origin platforms typically require additional integration layers or manual processes for these.

2. Data Protection (KVKK) — Increasing Risk

The Personal Data Protection Authority (KVKK) audits have been focusing on HR data since 2025. For effective KVKK compliance, the software should be able to digitally record employee consent, produce data processing inventory (VEA) output, support deletion/anonymization workflows, and store data access logs. All of these features are standard in CADRO.

3. Payroll Automation — Zero Error Tolerance

The 2026 minimum wage (gross 26,005.50 TL), changing social security premium ceiling, and new income tax brackets make payroll calculations complex. Automatic application of legal updates is critical.

4-6. TMS, Leave, and ATS — Integrated or Add-on?

When leave management and recruitment (ATS) modules work integrated with the main platform, the employee database is managed from a single point. Data inconsistency risks increase when different systems are used.

7. Reporting — Beyond Headcount

HR reporting is no longer just about absence counts. Turnover rate, time-to-hire, gender-based pay analysis, and predictive attrition indicators are now standard expectations.

8. Price / Value Balance

Global platforms typically apply dollar-based pricing per employee, making TRY/USD exchange rate volatility problematic for budget planning. Local platforms offer TRY-based pricing, providing budget predictability — a significant advantage in the Turkish market.

How to Calculate Migration Costs

The real cost of changing software is not the license fee. Also account for: data migration (existing records, payroll history, leave balances), training time (HR team, managers, and employees), integration development (accounting, ERP connections), and parallel operation during the transition period. CADRO's customer success team offers free data migration and guided setup support.

Conclusion: 5 Check Questions for the Right Decision

  1. Does the software integrate directly with Turkey SGK e-Declaration API, or does it require manual export/import?
  2. Can you produce a data processing inventory output under KVKK?
  3. Are legal changes (minimum wage, social security ceilings) updated automatically?
  4. What is the Turkish-language customer support SLA level?
  5. Is pricing TRY-based, or exposed to exchange rate fluctuations?

The answers to these questions will reveal a platform truly adapted to the Turkish market.
