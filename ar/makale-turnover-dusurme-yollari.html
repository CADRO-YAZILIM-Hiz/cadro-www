5 Data-Driven Ways to Reduce High Turnover Rates

Why do top talents resign within the first 90 days? Research consistently points to the same root causes: poor onboarding, lack of clear goals, invisible career paths, and perceived unfairness in evaluations. Each of these is preventable with the right data and systems.

1. Fix the Onboarding Experience

The leading cause of early departures is bad onboarding. When day one is chaos, trust evaporates instantly. A digital onboarding flow assigns tasks automatically to all departments.

2. Build Fairness with Data

When promotions and bonuses follow competency matrices, OKRs, and 360-degree feedback — not managerial bias — perceptions of favoritism disappear. Where fairness is proven, turnover drops dramatically.

3. Knowledge Hub & Transparency

When employees must constantly ask HR for policies, leave rights, or benefits, friction grows. A centralized knowledge base democratizes information and builds trust.
