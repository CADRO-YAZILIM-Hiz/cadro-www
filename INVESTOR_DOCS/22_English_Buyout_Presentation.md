# CADRO HR — English Buyout Presentation

**Purpose:** A buyer-facing presentation that sells the value of the product without exposing sensitive IP  
**Audience:** Strategic buyer, customer, or acquisition partner  
**Confidentiality:** External / Redacted View

---

## Slide 1 — Cover

**Title:** CADRO HR

**Subtitle:** A multilingual, mobile-first HR platform built for SME operations and strategic scale-up

**On-slide message:**
- Ready-to-buy product base
- Clear revenue engine
- Fast market expansion path

**Speaker Notes:**
Open with the core thesis: this is not a concept, it is a working product with a clear commercial path. Frame the conversation around buying time, reducing risk, and accelerating market entry. Emphasize that the buyer is acquiring a platform, not a blank sheet.

---

## Slide 2 — The Problem

**Title:** Why the market still hurts

**On-slide message:**
- HR teams still rely on spreadsheets, email chains, and disconnected tools
- Manual work creates errors, delays, and poor visibility
- Global products often miss local language and workflow needs

**Speaker Notes:**
Make the pain concrete. SMEs do not fail because they lack software; they fail because software is fragmented or too heavy. Explain that the biggest gap is not features, it is operational simplicity with local relevance.

---

## Slide 3 — What You Are Buying

**Title:** A working product, not a promise

**On-slide message:**
- Core HR workflows are already productized
- Billing and subscription logic are in place
- Product breadth is proven through live screens

**Visuals:**
- Dashboard screenshot
- Employee management screenshot
- Applicant tracking screenshot

**Speaker Notes:**
Use the screenshots as proof that the product is real and already shaped for daily operations. Keep the focus on the visible workflows rather than backend detail. The message is simple: the buyer gets a functioning base that can be improved, branded, or integrated.

---

## Slide 4 — Product Breadth

**Title:** Broad coverage across the employee lifecycle

**On-slide message:**
- Time and attendance
- Leave approval
- Performance and talent
- Settings and operational controls

**Visuals:**
- Leave management screenshot
- Performance screenshot
- System & company settings screenshot

**Speaker Notes:**
Show that CADRO is not a narrow tool. It touches the employee lifecycle from onboarding to performance and administration. This widens the commercial upside because the buyer is not purchasing one module; they are buying a platform footprint.

---

## Slide 5 — Live Market Presence

**Title:** Already live, already searchable

**On-slide message:**
- cadro.io is live in production
- Public product pages are already published
- The content structure is SEO-friendly and modular
- Language and glossary structure can expand into new countries quickly

**Speaker Notes:**
The market story matters. Explain that the public website is not just a brochure; it is a live acquisition and SEO asset. The content architecture supports future localization, so the buyer can add countries and languages without rebuilding the brand presence from zero.

---

## Slide 6 — Why This Wins Commercially

**Title:** The revenue engine is already modeled

**On-slide message:**
- Subscription pricing is established
- Upsell paths exist for payroll, AI, and integrations
- 25K USD increment scenarios have been modeled
- Base payback is around 10.2 months, aggressive case around 5.2 months

**Speaker Notes:**
Bring the discussion back to economics. The buyer should understand that this is not only a product acquisition; it is a revenue engine with measurable returns. Use the ROI scenario as a confidence-building anchor, not as a promise.

---

## Slide 7 — Safe to Evaluate, Safe to Buy

**Title:** External deck, protected core

**On-slide message:**
- Sensitive implementation details are intentionally redacted
- Architecture is presented at a high level only
- Controlled due diligence can follow under NDA

**Speaker Notes:**
This slide protects the IP while still showing seriousness. Make it clear that the presentation is intentionally non-invasive. The buyer can evaluate the business without receiving source-level disclosure too early.

---

## Slide 8 — Why It Should Be In The Market

**Title:** Why CADRO matters now

**On-slide message:**
- SMEs need simpler HR operations
- Multilingual coverage creates regional advantage
- The platform can expand through language packs and country content layers
- The buyer can accelerate reach rather than start from scratch

**Speaker Notes:**
This is where you sell the strategic logic. CADRO is relevant because it combines operational usefulness with regional expansion capability. Make the point that the language dictionary / localization layer can be extended quickly, opening new markets with lower incremental effort.

---

## Slide 9 — Buyout / Partnership Options

**Title:** How the deal can be structured

**On-slide message:**
- Full acquisition
- Exclusive license
- Strategic partnership with branding or white-label potential

**Speaker Notes:**
Be flexible but clear. The best buyer is not only buying code; they may be buying a growth lever. If they want full ownership, the path should be open. If they prefer a license or partnership first, that should also be possible.

---

## Slide 10 — Closing

**Title:** The takeaway

**On-slide message:**
CADRO is a ready-to-scale HR platform with visible product proof, live market presence, and a clear commercial upside. The buyer gets speed, focus, and reduced execution risk.

**Speaker Notes:**
Close on the core benefit: they do not need to rebuild the foundation. They can buy, brand, extend, and grow. End with a clear next step: review the data room, validate the commercial model, and discuss structure.
